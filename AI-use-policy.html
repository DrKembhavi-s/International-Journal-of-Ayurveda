Artificial Intelligence Use Policy

The International Journal of Ayurveda recognizes that artificial intelligence (AI) tools are increasingly used across research, writing, and editorial workflows. This policy sets out the journal's expectations for the disclosed and responsible use of AI by authors, reviewers, and editors.

1. For Authors

  • AI tools may be used to assist with language editing, literature searching, formatting, or data analysis, provided their use is disclosed in the Methods or Acknowledgments section.
  • AI tools must not be listed as an author. Authorship requires accountability for the work, which an AI tool cannot hold.
  • Authors remain fully responsible for the accuracy, originality, and integrity of all content, including any text, data, or citations generated with AI assistance.
  • AI-generated citations and references must be independently verified before submission; fabricated or inaccurate citations are treated as a research integrity violation.
  • AI tools must not be used to generate or manipulate research data, images, or results.

2. For Reviewers

  • Reviewers must not upload manuscripts, or any part of a manuscript, into public AI tools, as this may breach author confidentiality and violate the peer review agreement.
  • AI tools may be used only for personal reference or language support on the reviewer's own comments, not to generate the substantive review itself.

3. For Editors

  • Editors may use AI tools to support administrative tasks (e.g., correspondence drafting, formatting checks, plagiarism-screening triage), but all editorial decisions remain the responsibility of a named human editor.
  • Any AI tool used in the editorial workflow will be disclosed on the journal's Editorial Process page.

4. Disclosure Statement

The International Journal of Ayurveda discloses that AI-assisted tools are used in aspects of its own website development, content drafting, and editorial administration, under direct human review and final approval at every stage.
