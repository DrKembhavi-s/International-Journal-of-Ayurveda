Data Availability Policy

The International Journal of Ayurveda encourages authors to make the data underlying their research findings available, to the extent ethically and legally possible, in order to support transparency, reproducibility, and independent verification of results.

1. Requirements

  • Authors of original research articles must include a Data Availability Statement at the end of the manuscript, indicating whether the data is publicly available, available on reasonable request, or restricted, with the reason for any restriction stated.
  • Where possible, authors are encouraged to deposit raw data in a recognized public repository and cite the dataset with its DOI or accession number.
  • For clinical studies involving patient data, data sharing must comply with applicable ethical approvals, informed consent terms, and data protection regulations; de-identified data is preferred where sharing is possible.
  • Authors unable to share underlying data must provide a clear justification (e.g., ethical restriction, proprietary constraint, ongoing analysis) in the Data Availability Statement.
  • The journal reserves the right to request access to underlying data during the peer review or post-publication process in cases of reported concern.

2. Suggested Manuscript Statement Examples

"The datasets generated and analyzed during this study are available from the corresponding author on reasonable request."

"The data supporting the findings of this study are openly available in [repository name] at [DOI/link]."

"Data sharing is not applicable to this article as no new data were created or analyzed."
